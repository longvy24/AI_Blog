Do It Yourself

Start Yours This Weekend

You don't need a newsletter. You need to start the thing you keep not starting. The move is the same for almost any small business front:

  • Open Claude and hand it the blank slate. One line: what the business is and who it's for. "A meal-prep service for busy parents." "A Notion template shop for freelancers."
  • Let it name and brand it. Ask for a name, a tagline, and a simple logo direction. Reject what's off, keep what lands — you're the taste, it's the output.
  • Have it build the front. A signup or landing page as an Artifact, live to scroll. Then the first piece of content — issue one, first product, first post.
  • Ship the smallest real version. Don't wait for perfect. A name + a page + one thing to send is a launch. You can improve it by talking to Claude next weekend.
Why It Works

AI Collapses the Gap to Shipped

The reason most side hustles never launch isn't laziness — it's the distance between "idea" and "something real I can show people." That distance used to be weeks of unfamiliar work: design, copy, a website, the stuff you've never done. That gap is what AI collapses.

You describe the outcome — "a newsletter for side-hustlers" — and the model produces the brand, the page, the first draft. What's left isn't the intimidating part. It's the decisions only you can make: is this good, is this you, is this worth sending. The bottleneck was never the work. It was starting — and starting just got a lot cheaper.

! Still on you

The honest promise: AI demolishes the reason you haven't started. The setup, the brand, the blank page — gone in a weekend. The business itself — showing up, building an audience, earning the trust — is still yours to build. That's not the bad news. That's the only part that was ever really worth doing.

The One I Actually Built
