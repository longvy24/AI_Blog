The Real Reason

I Just Didn't Want the Hassle

Let me be honest — it wasn't the money. Getting a personal trainer is a whole production: finding one, the consult call, booking sessions, a standing commitment on your calendar. A plan actually built around you — your weight, your schedule, recalculated as you change — is genuinely worth it. I just couldn't be bothered to go set all that up. Classic me.

So I described the program I wanted to Claude instead. The result is Get Ripped: a 120-day fat-loss app with a five-day training split, four phases, and macros and meals that re-scale the moment I punch in my bodyweight. Claude wrote every line. Here's the whole build.

// The Pitch I Gave Claude
Build me a 120-day fat-loss program as a single web app. A 5-day training split across four phases. Macros and a meal rotation that recalculate to my bodyweight. Workout logging, progress charts, a supplement stack, and shopping lists. Everything in one file.

That was the seed. From there Claude built the whole thing — and I shaped it by using it. The training phases, the macro math, the meal rotation, the logging, the charts. I wrote zero lines of code. I brought the goals and the laziness; Claude was the entire engineering team.

"I punch in my weight, and everything reacts — my calories, my macros, my meals. The whole plan, tuned to me. That's the part you used to have to hire someone for."

Do It Yourself

Build the Plan Around You

You already know what you want — a plan that fits your body and your week, and adapts as you go. That's a description, not a degree in programming. Here's the loop:

  • Describe the goal and the constraints. "A 120-day cut." "Five days a week." "Macros that scale to my weight." The more specific your body and schedule, the more it's yours.
  • Ask for one runnable file first. Have Claude build the core — plan plus macro math — as a single file you can open in a browser today, before any extras.
  • Tune it by using it. Log a workout, punch in your weight, see if the numbers feel right. "Protein's too low." "Add a deload week." You feel the plan; Claude adjusts it.
  • Layer the extras last. Once the core works, ask for the shopping lists, the supplement stack, the charts. That's the difference between a spreadsheet and something you'll actually open on day 90.
Why It Works

The Real Barrier Was Friction

Training knowledge is basically free now — the barrier was never the information, and honestly it wasn't the money either. What actually stops most people is the friction: finding the coach, booking the consult, committing. What a good one really gives you is a plan built around you and adjusted as you change. That personalization — the math, the structure, the "recalculate everything because I'm down eight pounds" — is exactly what software is good at, and exactly what describing it to Claude gives you.

So the skill that matters isn't spreadsheet formulas or code — it's knowing what you want and being able to say it clearly. You don't need to hire anyone to get a plan that's yours. You just describe it once — about as lazy as it gets.

The Honest Part

What AI Builds and What You Do

No hype — building the app this way is genuinely doable in an afternoon. But an app is not a body. Here's the honest split.

✓ AI handles
  • All the code — the plan, the macro math, the logging, the charts.
  • Turning a description into a working app in an afternoon.
  • Recalculating everything the instant your weight changes.
  • Zero cost, zero install — it runs in a browser.
! Still on you
  • Actually doing the workouts — the app can't lift for you.
  • Knowing your body; a real coach adjusts to how you feel.
  • Getting a doctor's clearance before any serious training plan.
  • The discipline. The app is the easy part; showing up isn't.

The honest promise: AI removes the setup and the tooling wall completely — but it doesn't remove the reps. Get Ripped made the plan effortless to build. It does not make the training effortless. That part's still on me, and it always will be — which is exactly how it should work.
