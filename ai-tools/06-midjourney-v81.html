Two Corrections, Three Flags

Midjourney V8.1 is a real, well-documented release with meaningful improvements. The tool is not being misrepresented here the way earlier videos misnamed products. But the script's framing contains two factual errors and three unverifiable claims that need to be separated from the actual product.

// Correction — No Free Trial

What the script says: "Go to midjourney.com, start with a free trial."

What's accurate: Midjourney does not offer a free trial as of May 2026. It removed its free trial in 2023 due to demand. The cheapest entry point is the Basic plan at $10/month. There is no way to generate images without a paid subscription. Telling viewers to "start with a free trial" sends them to a paywall with no warning — that's a material error.

// Correction 2 — Video Shows $29, Script Says $10

What the script says: "For $10 a month."

What the video actually shows: At the pricing frame, Keyvello generated a browser mockup displaying "Low Monthly Price: $29 / Subscribe Now." The voiceover says $10 while the on-screen visual shows $29. This is a Keyvello generation error — the AI produced a price that contradicts the script. The correct Basic plan price is $10/month, but anyone who pauses on that frame sees $29. Neither price comes with a free trial.

"The tool is real. The 'free trial' isn't. And the video shows $29 while the voiceover says $10. Two pricing errors in one frame."

Scripted Claims

Three Lines To Flag

These three lines are scripted hook and proof devices — not personally verified claims. Documenting them here as committed in post 03.

// Flag 1 — "$500/hour Consultant"

What the script says: "I found out about it from a $500/hour consultant who casually mentioned it like it was obvious."

What this is: A narrative hook device generated by Claude. There is no $500/hour consultant. This is a storytelling technique to create credibility and FOMO — implying the tool is known by expensive insiders but not the general public. Midjourney has over 20 million registered users. It is not a secret.

// Flag 2 — "12 Lifestyle Images in Under 3 Minutes"

What the script says: "I generated 12 lifestyle product images in under 3 minutes. No model. No photographer. No studio."

What this is: A scripted proof claim, not a personally measured result. With V8.1's 4–5x speed improvement, generating 12 images in under 3 minutes is technically plausible — but I did not run this test. This follows the same pattern as the "4 hours saved" claim in post 03 and the "3 sign-ups" claim in post 04. It is scripted copy.

// Flag 3 — "Indistinguishable from Real Photos"

What the script says: "They're indistinguishable from real photos."

What this is: A subjective claim that cannot be sourced. V8.1 does produce high-quality, photorealistic images — independent reviews confirm this is one of its genuine strengths, particularly for product and lifestyle photography contexts. Whether any specific output is "indistinguishable from real photos" depends on the prompt, the subject, and who is looking. The claim is not verifiable as stated.

The Tool

What Midjourney V8.1 Actually Is

Midjourney V8.1 launched on April 30, 2026 on midjourney.com and Discord, after a two-week alpha that started April 14. The script's "dropped in April 2026" framing is accurate. Here's what actually changed.

V8.1 is a polish release built on V8.0, which was itself the largest infrastructure rebuild in Midjourney's history. The headline changes from the official V8.1 Alpha announcement: HD mode is now 3x faster and 3x cheaper than V8.0 — cheap enough to run as the default. Standard resolution is 50% faster and 25% cheaper. The aesthetic returned to something closer to V7 after community pushback on V8.0's look.

Other confirmed V8.1 improvements: image prompts and image weights are back (they were missing in V8.0), a new Prompt Shortener activates when prompts exceed length limits, and an updated Describe feature returns longer, more detailed prompts matched to V8's prompting style. Moodboards and style references are described as "super stable" in V8.1, compared to V8.0 where they were unreliable.

Worth noting: independent testing of V8.0 (the version before V8.1) found it to be roughly on par with V7 and in some cases actually worse — particularly on realism and scene construction. V8.0 struggled with logical object placement and fine details. V8.1 addresses many of those issues through aesthetic restoration and stability improvements, but Midjourney's underlying diffusion-based approach still prioritizes creative and cinematic output over photographic precision. For pure photorealism, tools like Imagen 4 and Flux 2 Pro are currently rated higher by independent reviewers. Midjourney's advantage remains its personalization system, aesthetic control, and cinematic style — not raw accuracy.

The script's claim that marketers use it for ad creative, product mockups, and social content is accurate and well-supported. V8.1 performs strongest on product, brand, architectural, and fashion visuals where mood, lighting, and composition matter. The video frames showing product mockups, fashion imagery, and packaging are representative of legitimate use cases — though the visuals are Keyvello-generated illustrations, not real Midjourney output.

// Midjourney Pricing (May 2026)

Basic: $10/month (3.3 GPU hours) · Standard: $30/month · Pro: $60/month · Mega: $120/month. Standard and higher plans include Relax Mode for unlimited slower generation. Pro and Mega add stronger privacy. No free trial. No public API as of May 2026.
