All Ten Techniques

Each technique gets its own page: what it does, the exact prompt template, an input/output example, when to reach for it, and one pro tip.

01
Role-Based Prompting
Tell the model who to be before you tell it what to do. Single highest-leverage one-line change.
02
Few-Shot Prompting
Show 2–3 examples first. The model pattern-matches your format almost exactly.
03
Chain-of-Thought
Three words that make AI reason instead of guess. Cuts errors on math and logic in half.
04
Iterative Refinement
Stop editing AI output. Edit your prompt instead. Four rounds beats one shot every time.
05
Delimiters
Wrap pasted content in triple quotes. The model stops confusing your instructions with your data.
06
Instructional Prompting
Start every prompt with a verb. Summarize, Analyze, Write. Models follow commands, not hints.
07
Negative Constraints
Tell AI what NOT to do. Removes preambles, jargon, and recap fluff in one line.
08
Prompt Chaining
Break complex tasks into sequential prompts. Quality compounds instead of degrading.
09
Retrieval Augmentation (RAG)
Paste your source. Tell the model to use only that. Hallucinations stop.
10
Emotion Prompting
Add stakes to your prompt. Real research shows it changes output depth.
